Rate a product by barcode

The very first use case from the original 2014 white book: consumers giving feedback on the products they buy — no store account, no manufacturer's permission. Every product barcode is a URI, and everything with a URI deserves feedback.

This is not a mock. This page publishes to a live Freedback demo server with ephemeral storage — it wakes from idle in a few seconds and may reset. Your feedback is signed by a key generated in your browser, and you can delete it (the ×): the right to erasure, implemented.

Enter or scan a barcode

Type the digits printed under any product barcode (EAN-13, UPC-A, EAN-8 or GTIN-14). The check digit is verified before anything is looked up.


Camera scanning needs Chrome/Android (the BarcodeDetector API) — typing the digits works everywhere.

Feedback on this product

That URI is a GS1 Digital Link — the product's standard identity on the web — so everyone scanning the same product converges on the same feedback.

How this works

The digits are validated with the standard GS1 mod-10 check digit, normalized to a 14-digit GTIN, and turned into https://id.gs1.org/01/<gtin14>. Three shipped widgets are then bound to that URI — stars on the product itself, comments and tags on #comments / #tags fragments so the counts never mix — reading from and publishing to the live server's /annotations/ endpoint with your self-signed P-256 identity.
